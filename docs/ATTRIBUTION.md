# Project: "Googol" — Comprehensive Intellectual Property Protection Policy

**1. Scope and Application**
- All above terms/variations are covered by layered protections: copyright, patent (utility and design), trademark (common law and registered), trade secret, and contractually imputed license terms.
- Policy supersedes, and is incorporated by reference into, all codebases, documentation, marketing, collaborations, and API endpoints branded as or related to "Googol," regardless of jurisdiction.

**2. Priority Claim and Authorship**
- All creative works, both hardware (mask works, layouts, schematics) and software (source, object, firmware, APIs, scripts, models), are authored by their respective designated inventors with documentation, timestamps, Zero-Width Signature watermarks, and chain-of-custody logs.
- All significant project contributions must be mapped to a verifiable, timestamped record, reinforcing inventorship and right to first claim.

**3. Use, Attribution, and Modification**
- No person or entity may copy, reproduce, reverse-engineer, license, distribute, adapt, or create derivatives of protected terms/artefacts without explicit, signed authorization from Project: Googol stewards (SuperLiquid.INC, Jacob Scott Farmer, et al.).
- All forks, derivatives, compatible plugins, and third-party integrations must preserve and display original attribution. Removal or obfuscation of attribution constitutes immediate and willful infringement.

**4. Automated Monitoring & Enforcement**
- All repositories, binaries, APIs, and published deliverables must embed cryptographic attribution (Zero-Width Unicode signatures, SPDX license headers, binary and steganographic watermarks).
- Automated monitoring scripts run continuously (code, doc, model, binary levels). Detection of infringement triggers: 
  - Automated build halt, quarantine, and forensic evidence capture
  - Blockchain timestamping of violation
  - Immediate AI-generated cease-and-desist, legal notice, and, if unmet, escalation to injunction/damages and law enforcement as needed.[3][1]

**5. Global Compliance & Claims Enforcement**
- Policy incorporates all protections available under:
  - US Copyright Law
  - US Semiconductor Chip Protection Act (SCPA)
  - EU Layout-Designs of Semiconductor Products Directive 87/54/EEC
  - Washington Treaty on Intellectual Property in Respect of Integrated Circuits (IPIC Treaty)
  - The Patent Cooperation Treaty (PCT)
  - Trademark and unfair competition statutes of all major markets.[4][5][8]
- Any applicable counterparty licensing restrictions or collaborative obligations with Google, Gemini, Nvidia, Microsoft, T-Mobile, Mercury-AI, or others must acknowledge and reinforce these terms.

**6. Prohibited Conduct**
- Prohibited: Any use of “Googol,” “Nanoswarm,” or variations—alone or in part, visually/phonetically similar, or as an identifier for products, services, SDKs, APIs, datasets, or publications—without written, explicit permission.
- Prohibited: Mask work copying, chip de-packaging, firmware dumping, reverse-engineering, API scraping, unauthorized re-implementation, or any act exceeding licensed/contracted scope.

**7. Enforcement Mechanisms**
- Every significant document, repo, build, artifact must:
  - Maintain untouched SPDX license blocks and digital signatures.
  - Pass runtime and post-build fingerprint checks prior to shipment or deployment.
  - Log any attribution modification, removal, or derivative attempt in immutable audit trails.
- Policy violations result in: build halt, distribution lockdown, legal notification, damages claim, and publication of violation to shared compliance networks and possibly public LEDGER.[3]

**8. Review and Amendment**
- The terms and policies may be updated or expanded as law and the technological landscape evolves. All users, partners, or third parties are required to keep current with the latest published policy in the SuperLiquid.INC Official Policy & Compliance Repository.
